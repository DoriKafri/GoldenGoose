Venture Intelligence Engine

The Venture Intelligence Engine is Develeap's internal platform for discovering, evaluating, and prioritizing new business ventures. It continuously harvests signals from across the tech ecosystem, scores ideas using AI, and simulates thought-leader reactions to surface the most promising opportunities.

How It Works

  • Harvest — Automated pipelines pull signals from tech blogs, startup trackers, academic papers, and community discussions.
  • Score — Each venture is scored by Claude across four dimensions and combined with thought-leader consensus into a composite score (0–100).
  • Rank & Prioritize — Ventures are sorted by score, with voting and annotations from the team layered on top.
  • Act — High-scoring ventures move from backlog to active development; tech gaps are tracked until readiness signals appear.

Key Concepts

  • Dark Factory Model — Ventures must be buildable and operable by 1–2 engineers using heavy automation, AI code generation, and managed services.
  • Tech Gaps — When a venture scores below 8/10 on tech readiness, the missing technology is tracked as a gap with estimated availability and readiness signals.
  • Thought Leaders — A panel of real and simulated industry voices (e.g., Kelsey Hightower, DHH, Paul Graham) that react to each venture, providing a crowd-wisdom signal.

Dashboard Tabs

The dashboard organizes ventures into five tabs based on their category and strategy.

Ventures
Original product ideas generated through AI brainstorming and signal harvesting. These are net-new SaaS/tool concepts in the DevOps, MLOps, and cloud-native space. Each is scored on monetization, cashout ease, dark factory fit, and tech readiness.
Clone
Early-stage startups that can be replicated and outcompeted. The engine identifies stealth or pre-Series-A companies with interesting products, then evaluates whether Develeap could build a superior version faster using the dark factory model. Includes the target company, their funding status, and the clone time estimate.
Quick Flip
Build-to-sell ventures designed for rapid acquisition. These are small tools or plugins that solve a specific pain point for a larger platform company. Includes potential acquirers, estimated acquisition price range, and margin opportunity analysis showing pricing undercuts.
Customers
Acqui-hire and consulting upsell opportunities. These represent existing companies that could become customers for Develeap's consulting services or be potential acquisition targets. Tracks company info, potential deal size, and strategic fit.
Missing Piece
Plugins, extensions, and companion tools that solve a specific user pain point in leading ISV tools (Terraform, Kubernetes, Datadog, Jenkins, etc.) without requiring users to switch platforms. Each entry identifies the target ISV, the pain point, and the integration approach. These are add-on products that ride the ISV's existing ecosystem and user base.
Training
Course and workshop ideas for Develeap's training business. Each entry includes target salary range, job demand, course length, pricing, and required skills. Generated from market gaps in DevOps/cloud-native education.

Pipeline Steps

  • 1. Signal Collection — The harvester fetches new content from all configured sources, filtering by the domain keywords defined in Settings (e.g., kubernetes, devops, mlops, gitops).
  • 2. Deduplication — Signals are compared against existing ventures to avoid creating duplicates. Similar ideas are merged or linked.
  • 3. Venture Generation — Claude analyzes each batch of signals and generates structured venture proposals with title, summary, problem statement, proposed solution, target buyer, and domain classification.
  • 4. Categorization — Ventures are automatically categorized into the appropriate tab (Venture, Clone, Quick Flip, Customer, or Training) based on their characteristics.
  • 5. Scoring — Each new venture is immediately scored across all four dimensions (see Scoring section).
  • 6. TL Simulation — Thought leaders are asked to react to the new ventures, providing upvote/neutral/downvote signals with reasoning.

Scheduled Jobs

  • Harvest + Score — Runs every N hours (default: 4). Collects signals, generates ventures, and scores them.
  • Tech Gap Check — Runs daily at a configured hour (default: 08:00 UTC). Re-evaluates ventures with tech gaps to see if readiness signals have appeared.
  • TL Signal Sync — Runs every N hours (default: 12). Refreshes thought-leader reactions and checks for real-world signals.
  • Training Brainstorm — Runs weekly on a configured day (default: Sunday). Generates new training course ideas.

Manual Harvest

You can trigger a harvest manually at any time using the "Trigger Harvest" button in the sidebar. This runs the full pipeline immediately regardless of the schedule.

Scoring Process

Every venture is evaluated by Claude across four core dimensions, then combined with thought-leader consensus into a single composite score on a 0–100 scale.

Scoring Dimensions (0–10 each)

Monetization
Revenue potential, market size, willingness to pay, and pricing power. How big is the addressable market and how strong is the business model?
Cashout Ease
Speed to first revenue. Low barrier to first sale, short sales cycles, self-serve potential, and land-and-expand opportunity.
Dark Factory Fit
Can 1–2 engineers build and run this with AI-assisted development, managed infra, and heavy automation? Penalizes anything requiring large teams or complex ops.
Tech Readiness
Is the technology to build this available today? Scores 8+ mean fully buildable now. Below 8 triggers a tech gap record tracking what's missing.

Thought Leader Score

A fifth dimension derived from simulated (and real) thought-leader reactions. Each leader votes upvote (1.0), neutral (0.5), or downvote (0.0). Real signals are weighted higher than simulated ones (configurable in Settings).

Composite Formula

composite = (
  monetization × wmon
  + cashout_ease × wcash
  + dark_factory_fit × wdf
  + tech_readiness × wtech
  + tl_score × wtl
) × 10

Default weights: Monetization 30%, Cashout Ease 25%, Dark Factory Fit 20%, Tech Readiness 15%, TL Score 10%. All weights are adjustable in Settings and must sum to 1.0.

Score Ranges

70+ — High potential 40–69 — Worth watching <40 — Low priority

Most ventures land in the 40–70 range. Scores above 70 indicate strong opportunities across all dimensions. The scoring model is intentionally conservative — only truly exceptional ideas get 80+.

Tech Gap Tracking

When a venture scores below 8/10 on tech readiness, the system records:

  • Gap Description — What technology is missing or immature
  • Missing Technology — The specific capability that's lacking
  • Estimated Availability — When it might become available
  • Readiness Signal — What event would indicate the gap is closing

Tech gaps are re-checked daily and resolved automatically when readiness signals are detected.

Thought Leaders

The thought leader system provides a crowd-wisdom layer on top of AI scoring. A curated panel of industry voices evaluates each venture, producing a consensus signal that influences the composite score.

How It Works

  • Simulated Reactions — Claude adopts the persona of each thought leader (based on their known opinions, writing style, and investment thesis) and generates a vote with reasoning.
  • Real Signals — When a thought leader actually tweets, blogs, or speaks about a topic related to a venture, that real signal is captured and weighted more heavily (default: 2x vs 1x for simulated).
  • Vote Types — Each leader votes upvote, neutral, or downvote with an explanation of why.
  • Social Links — Click on any thought leader card to see links to their real social profiles and content.

YC Compatibility

Ventures that receive upvotes from Y Combinator-affiliated thought leaders (Paul Graham, Garry Tan, Michael Seibel, Dalton Caldwell, Jared Friedman) are flagged as "YC Compatible" with a special badge, indicating the idea aligns with YC's investment thesis.

Signal Weights

The relative importance of real vs. simulated signals is configurable in Settings under the Scoring section. By default, real reactions carry 2x the weight of simulated ones, reflecting higher confidence in actual market signals.

Suggest a Venture

Anyone can propose a new venture idea using the "+ Suggest" button in the sidebar. The suggest feature adapts to whichever tab you're currently viewing.

Submission Fields

  • Title — A concise name for the venture or idea.
  • Summary — A brief description of what the product/service does.
  • Problem — What pain point does this solve?
  • Proposed Solution — How would this be built or delivered?
  • Target Buyer — Who would pay for this?
  • Domain — Which domain category it falls under (DevOps, MLOps, etc.).

What Happens After Submission

  • The venture is added to the database with status "backlog".
  • It is immediately scored by Claude across all four dimensions.
  • Thought leader reactions are simulated.
  • The composite score is calculated and the venture appears in its tab.

Voting & Annotations

Team members can upvote or downvote any venture and leave comments. The vote count appears alongside the AI score to provide a human signal. Anonymous identities are assigned automatically so voting stays lightweight and fun.

Settings

The Settings panel (gear icon in sidebar) lets you customize all core parameters of the engine. Changes are persisted to the database and take effect immediately.

Setting Categories

  • AI / Claude — Choose the Claude model, set temperature for scoring vs. ideation, and configure token limits.
  • Scoring — Adjust the five dimension weights (must sum to 1.0) and configure real vs. simulated signal multipliers. Use "Normalize" to auto-balance weights.
  • Harvester — Set how often the harvest pipeline runs, when daily gap checks happen, TL sync frequency, brainstorm count, and training day.
  • Domains — Edit the keyword list used for filtering signals, toggle active domain categories, and manage company blog RSS feeds.
  • Notifications — Configure thresholds for high-score alerts, popular vote notifications, and weekly digest preferences.
  • Display — Set page size and default sort order for the venture list.

Defaults & Resets

Every setting has a built-in default. The database only stores your overrides. Use "Reset Section" to restore all settings in the current category to their defaults. Settings that differ from defaults are highlighted in the UI.
